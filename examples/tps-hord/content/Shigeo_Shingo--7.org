** Notes

Shigeo Shingo (新郷重夫, 1909–1990) was a Japanese
industrial engineer who contributed significantly to the
Toyota Production System, particularly in the areas of
poka-yoke (mistake-proofing) and SMED (Single-Minute
Exchange of Die — rapid changeover techniques).

While Taiichi Ohno is credited as the system's primary
architect, Shingo developed many of the practical
mechanisms that make TPS work on the factory floor.  His
poka-yoke approach — designing fixtures and processes so
that errors are physically impossible — is one of the
most widely adopted TPS concepts outside Toyota.
