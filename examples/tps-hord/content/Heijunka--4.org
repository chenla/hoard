** Notes

Heijunka (平準化) means "leveling" — the practice of
smoothing the type and quantity of production over a fixed
period.  Rather than building products in large batches
(all of type A, then all of type B), heijunka distributes
different product types evenly throughout the day.

This leveling is essential to making Just-in-Time work.
Without heijunka, upstream processes face wildly
fluctuating demand, which forces them to maintain large
inventories.  With leveling, demand on every upstream
process becomes stable and predictable.

A heijunka box (a visual scheduling tool divided into a
grid of time slots and product types) is the physical
mechanism used to implement production leveling on the
shop floor.
