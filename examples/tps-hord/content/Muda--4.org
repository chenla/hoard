** Notes

Muda (無駄) means "waste" or "futility" in Japanese.  In
the Toyota Production System, Taiichi Ohno identified
seven types of muda:

1. Transport — unnecessary movement of materials
2. Inventory — excess stock beyond immediate need
3. Motion — unnecessary movement of people
4. Waiting — idle time between process steps
5. Overproduction — making more than needed
6. Overprocessing — doing more work than required
7. Defects — effort spent on rework and scrap

Overproduction is considered the worst form of muda
because it generates all other forms of waste.

Muda is one of three related concepts in TPS: muda
(waste), mura (unevenness), and muri (overburden).
Together they describe the sources of inefficiency that
the production system aims to eliminate.
