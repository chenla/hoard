# Jidoka—4

Jidoka (自働化) is one of the two pillars of the Toyota
Production System, alongside Just-in-Time.  The concept
is often translated as "autonomation" — automation with a
human touch.

The principle originated with Sakichi Toyoda's automatic
loom, which could detect when a thread broke and
automatically stop.  This prevented defective cloth from
being produced and freed the operator to oversee multiple
looms.

In the TPS, jidoka means that machines and operators are
designed to detect abnormalities and stop production
immediately.  The key insight is that stopping to fix a
problem is cheaper than passing defects downstream.  This
is implemented through mechanisms like andon cords (which
workers pull to stop the line) and poka-yoke (error-
proofing devices).
