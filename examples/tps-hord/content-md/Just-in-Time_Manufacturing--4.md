# Just-in-Time Manufacturing—4

Just-in-Time (JIT) manufacturing is a production strategy
that strives to improve return on investment by reducing
in-process inventory and associated carrying costs.  In
JIT, parts arrive at each production stage precisely when
needed — not before, not after.

JIT is one of the two pillars of the Toyota Production
System, alongside Jidoka.  The system was developed by
Taiichi Ohno, who was inspired by American supermarkets
where shelves were restocked only as customers purchased
items.

The JIT approach requires: reliable supplier delivery,
small lot sizes, quick changeover times, and a level
production schedule (heijunka).  Kanban is the primary
signaling mechanism that makes JIT work in practice.
