# Genchi Genbutsu—4

Genchi genbutsu (現地現物, literally "actual place, actual
thing") is the practice of going to the source to observe
and understand a situation firsthand.  In the Toyota
Production System, this means managers and engineers must
go to the factory floor (the gemba) to see problems
directly rather than relying on reports or secondhand
information.

Taiichi Ohno was famous for drawing a chalk circle on the
factory floor and having engineers stand in it for hours,
observing the production process until they truly
understood what was happening.

The principle reflects a deep skepticism toward
abstraction: data and reports are maps, but the territory
is what actually happens on the shop floor.  Decisions
should be grounded in direct observation.
