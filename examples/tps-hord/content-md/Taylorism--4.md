# Taylorism—4

Taylorism, or scientific management, is an approach to
workflow management developed by Frederick Winslow Taylor
in the late 19th century.  It applies engineering
principles to labor: breaking tasks into small, timed
steps, training workers to perform each step optimally,
and separating planning from execution.

The Toyota Production System both builds on and rejects
Taylorism.  It adopts the systematic analysis of work
processes but rejects the separation of thinking from
doing.  In TPS, the workers on the line are expected to
observe, think, and improve — not just execute
instructions from above.  Kaizen is the anti-Taylorist
element of an otherwise highly systematic production
method.
