# W. Edwards Deming—7

W. Edwards Deming (1900–1993) was an American engineer,
statistician, and management consultant.  His lectures in
Japan in the 1950s on statistical process control and
quality management profoundly influenced the development
of Japanese manufacturing, including the Toyota
Production System.

Deming's key contributions include: the Plan-Do-Check-Act
(PDCA) cycle, the system of profound knowledge, and the
emphasis on reducing variation as the primary path to
quality.  His philosophy that quality is built into the
process — not inspected after the fact — is a direct
ancestor of the jidoka principle.

The Deming Prize, established in Japan in 1951, remains
one of the most prestigious quality awards.  Toyota won
it in 1965.
