# Kaizen—4

Kaizen (改善, literally "change for better") is the
philosophy of continuous incremental improvement.  In the
Toyota Production System, kaizen is not a program or
event — it is a daily practice where every worker is
expected to identify waste and suggest improvements.

The kaizen approach contrasts with large-scale innovation
(kaikaku).  Rather than dramatic overhauls, kaizen
focuses on small, sustainable changes that compound over
time.  A factory might make hundreds of tiny improvements
per month, each saving seconds or reducing one source of
waste.

The concept has spread far beyond manufacturing into
software development, education, personal productivity,
and organizational management.
